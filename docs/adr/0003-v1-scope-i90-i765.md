# Scope v1 to Form I-90 and Form I-765, excluding complex petitions

v1 supports only Form I-90 (Permanent Resident Card renewal/replacement) and Form I-765 (EAD — both initial and renewal) and explicitly excludes attorney matching, complex one-time petitions (I-130/I-485, asylum), forum/news, web, and monetization. These two are the highest-recurrence USCIS filings, which is what the retention thesis needs; supporting *initial* I-765 (not renewal-only) captures first-time filers who will later renew, serving that same thesis.

## Consequences

The data model must carry the initial-vs-renewal distinction on I-765 from day one, and "recurring renewals" marketing must coexist with first-time-filing support. I-130/I-485 are deliberate no-s despite being more common, because their complexity and low recurrence work against both the UPL boundary and the retention thesis.

## Amended (2026-07-01)

Scope unchanged; language updated. **Application** is the product unit (see CONTEXT.md) — "filing(s)" above reads as *applications*. The initial/renewal/replacement distinction is carried by the required `applicationKind` field on `applications`, and **replacement** is explicit in v1 alongside renewal and initial (Work Permit initial/renewal/replacement; Green Card renewal/replacement).
